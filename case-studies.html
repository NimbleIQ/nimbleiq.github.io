How a B2B SaaS Company Increased Pipeline ROI by 185% with Modern MMM

Situation

A fast-growing B2B SaaS company with $75M in annual revenue was struggling to optimize their $15M marketing budget across paid search, social, content marketing, and events. Their existing MMM was outdated and provided conflicting signals about channel performance.

Complication

Leadership needed to make Q4 budget decisions but had no confidence in their measurement. The sales team was pushing for more events budget while the demand gen team insisted paid channels were driving better ROI. Previous MMM showed unrealistic diminishing returns.

Question

How should they allocate their marketing budget to maximize pipeline generation while maintaining confidence in attribution? What were the true saturation curves for each channel?

Answer

We rebuilt their MMM using modern Bayesian techniques, incorporating proper lead-to-close timing, account-based marketing effects, and competitive pressures. The new model revealed that events were significantly undervalued in the previous model, while paid social had reached saturation.

Situation

A mid-market e-commerce company invested $5M in their Black Friday/Cyber Monday campaign across multiple channels but had no way to isolate the incremental impact from baseline seasonal effects and competitor activities.

Complication

With Cyber Monday results coming in, leadership needed to understand campaign effectiveness immediately to make decisions about holiday season spend. Traditional attribution was showing inflated results due to seasonality, while GA4 data was fragmented across touchpoints.

Question
